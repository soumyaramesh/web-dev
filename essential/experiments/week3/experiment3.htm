About the Experiment

Bootstrap allows for a responsive, fluid grid system that scales up to 12 columns corresponding to the increase or decrease of the viewport size. The Grid systems is used to create page layouts whose content is housed in the form of rows and columns. Rows must be placed within a .container (fixed-width) or .container-fluid (full-width) and content should be placed within columns. Predefined grid classes like .row and .col-xs-4 are available for making grid layouts. Grid columns are created by specifying the number of 12 available columns you wish to span. For example, three equal columns would use three .col-xs-4. Look at the examples below:
